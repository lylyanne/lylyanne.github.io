Pair Programming

by Ryan Milstead & Felicia Wang

What is pair programming?

Two programmers working together on a single project to create cleaner and more viable code.

Why is pair programming important to DBC?

Pair programming is vital to DBC so students can practice communicating and utilize the strengths of their fellow programmers.

Here are a few more reasons:

  • catch errors early
  • learn from your pair's strength
  • practice empathy

Compare Drive and Navigator

A Driver is the programmer who is writing the code under the direction of the navigator. However, the driver is free to give his opinion and ideas.

A Navigator is programmer who is directing the driver in the implementation of the code. They work with the driver to cultivate ideas and then help the driver to write them.

Reflection

This was a fun and challenging pairing session for us. This was our first time working together so we are not familiar with each other's work habits, communication skills, and level of expertise.

I, Felicia, feel like it was hard for me to voice my thoughts out loud and I had to resist the impulse of wanting to take over a few times.

I, Ryan, had some difficulty towards the end of the session becasue we were attempting to impliment some styling that I was not familiar with so I had to take a back seat and I could not contribute as much as I would have liked.
